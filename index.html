Purpose & Scope

Slate is an automated, data-informed system designed to facilitate effective decision-making, aligning with CUNY's modernization goal as outlined in the "CUNY Living New York: 2023-2030 Strategic Roadmap." The system emphasizes integrating people, processes, and technologies to keep prospective and current CUNY students at the center while supporting the diverse needs of various colleges.

With the initial implementation phase of Slate (the newest CRM platform) complete, the focus shifts to system stabilization and enhancement. This support model defines a clear, sustainable support structure for Slate at CUNY Central, ensuring campuses experience no drop-off in support as the implementation phase winds down. The central team serves as the product owner and expert authority on Slate, aligning campus innovation with university-wide strategy, governance, and data standards.

Immediate goals include strengthening yield, increasing retention, closing the parent engagement gap, and providing real-time, personalized portals for everyone from prospects to CUNY leadership. Additional goals involve aligning various platforms, specifically mentioning Freshdesk.

The proposed structure aims to achieve these goals through:

  • Development of policies and procedures
  • Establishment of decision-making and reporting mechanisms
  • Implementation of ongoing training and communication
  • A tiered support model that scales as adoption grows
  • High-resolution ticketing and governance processes for new requests, integrations, and enhancements

Guiding Principles

Expectation Setting

Be explicit about what is in scope, what is "not yet," and why.

Perceived Expertise

Central team members must be visible as Slate experts (leading trainings, answering advanced questions, owning decisions).

Tiered Escalation

Issues move from campus to central functional leads to central product ownership only when needed.

Strategic Alignment

Campus innovations should align with the common data model, security standards, and CUNY-wide priorities.

Data-Driven Decisions

Use ticket and cohort data (volume, categories, resolution time) to guide staffing, training, and process changes.
